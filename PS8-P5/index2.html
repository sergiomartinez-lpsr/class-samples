The Best Soccer Players of All time

3. Pele

Pele is known as one of the greatest to ever play the game. The Brazilian has scored more than 1000 goals in his soccer career. He made his professional debut at the age of just 15 with Brazilian club Santos. Pele has been the only player in the history of soccer to have won 3 Fifa World Cup Medals. After a very succesful career, Pele retire in 1977 at the age of 36. His last game was with the New York Cosmos against the team that watched him grow, Santos.

2. Ronaldinho

During his time at FC Barcelona, Ronaldinho was the best player in the world and arguably the best player in the history. During his prime, Ronaldinho won absolutely everything with his club and international team. He won the Fifa World Cup with Brazil in 2002 and the UEFA Champions League with Barcelona in 2006. His great performances got him to win the Ballon D'or in 2005. Ronaldinho left Barcelona in 2008 to join Italian club AC Milan. Since then Ronaldinho has played for Flamengo, Atletico Mineiro, Queretaro, Fluminense, and is currently a free agent looking for a team.

1. Sergio Martinez

For Martinez, everything started when he was playing for Escuela de Futbol Tarimoro. Playing as a centermid, Sergio won the Guanajuato State Cup and was a runner up for Nationals. He played for a while at the FC Celaya Academy where he won one title. At 14 years old, he transfered to Richmond Sol Cobras on a $200 dollar transfer. In his first season with Cobras U15, he won U15 NorCal Premier League 2015. He finished the season with 4 goals and 6 assists in 10 games. Unfortunately, injuries have had a big role on Sergio's career. In July of 2015, a torned quadriceps stopped him for 3 weeks from playing. Also in December of 2015, an inflamed appendix caused him to play only one game in the Ajax U15 College Showcase. Despite all his injuries, Sergio is without a doubt up there with the greatest of all time.
